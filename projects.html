Key research contribution

Research Question

Does SeqA-mediated sister chromatid cohesion modulate the efficiency and fidelity of homologous recombination-mediated DSB repair by influencing the spatial proximity of homologous sequences?

We investigate whether prolonged chromatid cohesion at 'snap' loci facilitates faster and more accurate homology search during DSB repair compared to 'non-snap' loci with rapid segregation.

Key Findings

  • DSBs at snap loci (e.g., gln) showed significantly higher survival than non-snap loci (e.g., dnaB).
  • SeqA deletion eliminated the repair advantage at snap loci, confirming its critical role in cohesion.
  • Time-lapse imaging showed faster RecA-GFP disappearance at gln, indicating rapid and efficient repair.
  • Longer and brighter RecA filaments formed at snap loci, supporting enhanced homology search efficiency.
  • RecB dependency confirmed homologous recombination as the repair mechanism.

Figure 1: Repair kinetics and survival comparison between gln (snap) and dnaB (non-snap) loci. DSB induction at gln showed significantly higher survival, with accelerated restoration of copy number and decreased RecA-GFP lifespan.

Figure 2: Time-lapse imaging of RecA filament dynamics post DSB induction. Snap loci support rapid filament resolution; non-snap loci show delayed RecA clearance and fragmented filament structures.

Figure 3: Fluorescent micrographs show stronger, extended RecA filaments at gln compared to compact foci at dnaB, confirming spatial differences in filament stability and repair efficiency.

Figure 4: Schematic model summarizing how SeqA-mediated chromatid cohesion promotes efficient homology search and faithful HR-based DSB repair at snap loci.
