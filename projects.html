SeqA Mediated DNA Repair Modulation

This study explores how sister loci cohesion duration in E. coli affects homologous recombination (HR)-mediated DNA double-strand break (DSB) repair efficiency and fidelity. The E. coli chromosome exhibits prolonged cohesion at snap loci on the right replichore, regulated by SeqA. DSBs induced at the snap locus (gln) resulted in higher cell survival compared to the non-snap locus (dnaB), attributed to faster repair, as indicated by earlier restoration of locus copy number and shorter RecA-GFP lifespan. Time-lapse imaging revealed longer and more stable RecA filaments at gln compared to dnaB, which were compromised in ΔseqA strains. Mutation accumulation analysis showed higher mutation frequencies at dnaB under normal conditions and in the presence of sub-MIC ciprofloxacin, suggesting lower repair fidelity at non-snap loci. Additionally, in silico analysis of 94 natural E. coli isolates revealed higher sequence conservation at snap loci, supporting the hypothesis that prolonged sister loci cohesion enhances repair efficiency and maintains genomic integrity. These findings demonstrate that locus-specific cohesion duration plays a crucial role in determining the kinetics, fidelity, and outcome of HR-mediated DSB repair, with broader implications for genome stability and mutation accumulation.
