SeqA-Mediated Cohesion Enhances DSB Repair Efficiency

Homologous recombination repair is spatially influenced by chromosomal organization in E. coli

Research Hypothesis

Does SeqA-mediated sister chromatid cohesion modulate the efficiency and fidelity of HR-mediated DSB repair by influencing the spatial proximity of homologous sequences?

Background

In E. coli, certain chromosomal loci called “snap loci” exhibit prolonged post-replication cohesion. We hypothesize that this cohesion facilitates faster and more accurate homologous recombination repair by spatially aligning sister chromatids.

Experimental Design

  • Used I-SceI system to induce site-specific DSBs at snap (gln) and non-snap (dnaB) loci.
  • Tracked survival via CFUs and monitored RecA-GFP dynamics through time-lapse microscopy.

Key Findings

  • Higher survival observed at snap loci (gln) vs. non-snap loci (dnaB).
  • SeqA knockout abolishes this repair advantage, confirming its cohesion role.
  • RecA filaments at snap loci are brighter, longer, and resolve faster.
  • HR is RecB-dependent at both loci, validating the repair pathway.
