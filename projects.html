Spatial Control of DNA Repair by SeqA

Precision homology search guided by chromosomal organization

Key Results

  • Site-specific DSBs induced via I-SceI at gln-psd (snap) and dnaB-rfaj (non-snap).
  • Cell survival tracked using CFU assays.
  • RecA-GFP monitored using time-lapse microscopy.

Figure 1: Snap loci exhibit faster repair kinetics and greater survival than non-snap loci.

Figure 2: Time-lapse dynamics of RecA filament formation and resolution show enhanced speed at snap loci.

Figure 3: Representative images of RecA filaments. Snap loci show continuous, elongated filaments vs. fragmented structures at non-snap loci.

“Snap loci act as spatial anchors, ensuring chromosomal symmetry and high-fidelity repair through SeqA-mediated cohesion.”
