About

I am a molecular microbiologist with a Ph.D. from Jamia Millia Islamia, currently working as a Scientist at Micro CRISPR Pvt. Ltd., New Delhi.

My research lies at the intersection of bacterial chromosome regulation, global transcriptional control, and stress-response pathways, with a particular focus on how these processes shape antimicrobial resistance and cellular phenotypes in Escherichia coli.

During my doctoral work, I investigated the role of SeqA-mediated chromosome cohesion in DNA repair, replication dynamics, and antibiotic stress adaptation. This work contributed to peer-reviewed publications and highlighted how transcriptional rewiring can drive low-level drug resistance.
